Route Alerts
⚠ Wildlife Reserve Traffic Restrictions

Bandipur and Mudumalai (Leg 1) are closed to all traffic between 9 PM and 6 AM. Maximum speed inside reserves: 30 km/h. Forest rangers actively enforce with radar. Any violation risks a fine and escorted exit.

📋 Required Documentation

Always have ready: motorcycle licence, RC (Registration Certificate), valid insurance, Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate, and passport or Aadhaar. At forest checkpoints an entry permit may be required — verify with the relevant state forest department beforehand.

Police & Forest Checkpoints
Leg 1
KUTTA CHECKPOINT — Nagarhole Forest Corridor
State border crossing between Wayanad (Kerala) and Coorg (Karnataka) at the edge of Nagarhole National Park. Guards verify vehicle documents and usually ask your destination.
⚡ TACTIC: Have papers on top of your bag before you arrive. Be quick and confident. Say "Ooty" as your destination. No lingering.
Leg 1
BANDIPUR TIGER RESERVE — Entry & Exit Gates
North gate (from Gundlupet) and south gate (into Mudumalai). Vehicle registration required. Nocturnal closure 9 PM–6 AM strictly enforced — plan your crossing for daylight. With 11 AM departure from Kalpetta you'll cross Bandipur around 2 PM — well within the window.
⚡ TACTIC: Maintain 30 km/h strictly — rangers use radar. Do not stop outside designated areas. Have passport and RC visible and accessible.
Leg 1
MUDUMALAI TIGER RESERVE — Nilgiris Biosphere
Second reserve in the Nilgiris corridor. Same procedure as Bandipur. Road can be blocked 15–30 minutes when elephants or tigers are spotted crossing.
⚡ TACTIC: Build buffer time into this leg. Never accelerate inside the reserve under any circumstance.
Leg 3
MALAKKAPPARA — The Forbidden Route Checkpoint
Deep in the Anaimalai–Sholayar corridor at the Tamil Nadu/Kerala border. Registration is mandatory — name, vehicle number, and destination. This is the gateway to the wildest leg of the loop.
⚡ TACTIC: They rarely inspect the bike. They just want to know who enters and exits the deep forest. Be polite, direct, and cooperative. Destination: "Munnar, Kerala."
Leg 4
THENI TOWN POLICE — Avoid City Centre
Theni is known for active speed traps and document checks on the main highway. Not a forest checkpoint — routine urban traffic enforcement.
✅ ALTERNATIVE: Rural bypass roads south of Theni. Adds ~15 min but skips the city entirely and avoids police presence.
Leg 6
NILAMBUR ROUTE — Preferred Re-entry to Wayanad
Return via Nilambur and the teak forests enters Wayanad from the south. Much less police presence than the main Karnataka highways. A calmer, more beautiful finish to the loop.
✅ RECOMMENDED return route. UNESCO heritage teak corridor, minimal traffic, spectacular close to the loop.

Waterfalls on Route
💧

Irupu Falls — Off Route (optional)

  • Location: Nagarhole, Coorg, Karnataka
  • Height: ~60m
  • Detour from Kalpetta area · +8 km · not on main loop route
  • Best: Jun–Sep (monsoon season)
  • Entry fee: ₹30 per person
💧

Monkey Falls — Leg 3

  • Location: Foot of Valparai climb, TN
  • Height: ~15m — intimate & beautiful
  • Access: Directly off the highway
  • Warning: macaques steal food
  • No entry fee, park roadside
💧

Athirappilly Falls — Leg 3

  • Location: Chalakudy River, Kerala
  • Height: ~80m — largest in Kerala
  • Known as the "Niagara of India"
  • Entry fee: ₹60 per person
  • Crowded weekends — arrive early
🔥 Theyyam — March Opportunity
🔥 March is peak Theyyam season — you are riding through it

Theyyam is a sacred ritual art performed Dec–April at temples and family shrines (kavus) across North Kerala. It is not a show — it is a live divine ritual. March is one of the most active months. Your Leg 6 route passes through Nilambur and Mananthavady, which are in the Theyyam zone. Performances often start at dusk or midnight and run until dawn.

🔥

What is Theyyam

  • Ancient ritual art of North Kerala
  • Performer embodies a deity or ancestor spirit
  • 400+ varieties — each unique costumes & makeup
  • Performed at kavus (sacred groves) and temples
  • Free to attend — respectful observation expected
📍

Where on your route

  • Nilambur (Leg 6) — Malappuram district kavus
  • Mananthavady (Leg 6) — Wayanad North shrines
  • Kalpetta area — ask your accommodation
  • Any village heading north toward Kannur
  • Core zone: Kannur & Kasaragod (~2h north of Kalpetta)
⚠

Important — Read This

  • Dates change year-to-year (Malayalam calendar)
  • No fixed schedule — confirm locally
  • Often starts at dusk or midnight, runs to dawn
  • Arrive quietly, observe respectfully
  • Photography: ask permission first
🔗
